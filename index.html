About

I am an Assistant Professor of Economics in the Department of Economics, Business, and Finance at Lake Forest College. I received my Ph.D. in Economics from Cornell University in 2020.

My research spans macroeconomics, international finance, and applied policy work in development macroeconomics. I am particularly interested in issues related to sovereign debt in emerging markets, monetary policy, and the intersection of economic development and financial systems, with a regional focus on Sub-Saharan Africa and Ethiopia.

I also hold an M.P.A. in International Development from the Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University and an M.Sc. in Economics from the University of Copenhagen.

Research Interests: Macroeconomics · International Finance · Monetary Economics
